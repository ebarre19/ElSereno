Pre 1769

Before El Sereno was discovered and settled, the Native American village of Otsungna was located on the banks of a stream that would later be named Arroyo Rosa de Castilla. It ran east of present-day Guardia and Farsnworth avenues.

There was a trail that conencted the Otsungna village to Yangna village that was located on the Los Angeles River. This trail also conencted to village of Sibagna, which was near the future site of the Mission of San Gabriel. This route would later become Mission Road.

Although little evidence exist today, this is a result of the area never been seriously examined nor were items preserved. Unfortunately, any surviving evidence that remained was obliterated during the development of the 710 freeway and during the construction of Cal State University Los Angeles.

Spanish Exploration

When the spanish explored the area that would be El Sereno, they built and comminsed what would become part of San Gabriel Mission land. Witn Mexican rule the ownership went to Juan Ballesteros, who named his land Rancho Rosa de Castilla.


The El Sereno area, then known as the Mission Hills was part of the lands owned by the Mission San Gabriel Archangel, founded in 1771. Used for cattle grazing, an adobe was constructed here in 1776 by vaqueros from the Mission.
